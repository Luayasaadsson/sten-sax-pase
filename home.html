How to play

Rock, Paper & Scissors
Welcome to our interactive "Rock, Paper, Scissors" game! Here's how you can play and enjoy the game:

Starting the Game

When you open the game, you will see a display with options for Rock, Paper, and Scissors, represented by their respective icons. The game begins with no hands displayed on the screen, providing a clean and straightforward interface.

Selecting Your Move

Use the joystick control (on-screen or physical, if applicable) to cycle through the hand gestures for Rock, Paper, or Scissors. Press 'j' on your keyboard to change the hand gesture. The selected gesture will be displayed on the screen. You can also bend the joystick image by clicking on it, providing a more interactive experience.

Initiating a Round

Once you’ve made your choice, press 'g' to start the round. An animation will play, adding excitement to the reveal of both your hand and the robot’s hand.

Understanding the Outcome

The game will automatically determine the winner based on the classic rules: Rock crushes Scissors, Scissors cuts Paper, and Paper covers Rock. Wins, losses, and draws are tracked and displayed on the screen.

Using Special Features

Press 'F' or 'f' to toggle a cheat mode, altering the game's behavior. You can reset the game and all scores at any point by pressing 'r'.

Sound and Music

Enjoy different sound effects for actions like switching hands, starting a round, and winning or losing. You can select music to play during the game from a dropdown menu for a customized experience.

Ending a Game

The game tracks the number of wins and losses. Once a set limit is reached, an alert will declare the game over. The game will automatically reset after the alert, so you can start a new round immediately.

Accessibility and Controls

Keyboard shortcuts for easy navigation and gameplay. The game is designed with intuitive controls for a seamless user experience.

Rules

Rock, Paper & Scissors

Gestures

Rock: Closed fist.
Scissors: Index and middle fingers extended. Paper: Open hand, palm facing down.

To play

Count to three (usually "rock, paper, scissors!") and show a gesture on three. Rock crushes scissors, scissors cuts paper, and paper covers rock.

Winner

If the same gesture, it's a draw. Otherwise, see which gesture beats the other.

Usage

A fun and fair way to make quick decisions.
